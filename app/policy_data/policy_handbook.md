# Motor Insurance Policy Handbook

## Liability Coverage
Liability coverage pays for bodily injury and property damage that the policyholder
causes to others in an at-fault accident. It does not cover damage to the
policyholder's own vehicle. Minimum liability limits are set by state law; this
policy provides limits as stated on the declarations page.

## Collision Coverage
Collision coverage pays to repair or replace the policyholder's vehicle after a
collision with another vehicle or object, regardless of fault. The policyholder's
deductible applies before any payout. Collision coverage does not apply if the
policy has lapsed or if the vehicle was being used for commercial ride-share or
delivery purposes at the time of the accident, unless a rideshare endorsement is
on file.

## Comprehensive Coverage
Comprehensive coverage pays for damage to the policyholder's vehicle from causes
other than a collision: theft, vandalism, fire, falling objects, hail, flooding,
and collisions with animals. The deductible applies. Comprehensive coverage
includes glass and windshield damage unless a separate glass endorsement with a
zero deductible is on file.

## Exclusions
This policy does not cover: damage from intentional acts by the policyholder;
damage occurring while the vehicle is being operated by an unlisted or excluded
driver; damage occurring while driving under the influence; wear-and-tear,
mechanical breakdown, or damage from lack of maintenance; racing or competitive
driving events; damage occurring outside the policy's effective coverage period.

## Deductibles
The deductible is the amount the policyholder pays out of pocket before coverage
applies. Standard deductibles are $500 or $1,000 as selected at policy issuance
and shown on the declarations page. Deductibles apply once per claim, not per
item of damage.

## Total Loss Determination
A vehicle is declared a total loss when the cost of repair, plus salvage value,
meets or exceeds the vehicle's actual cash value (ACV) at the time of loss —
generally when repair costs exceed 70% of ACV. In a total loss, the payout is the
vehicle's ACV minus the deductible and any outstanding lienholder balance.

## Rental Car Reimbursement
If the policyholder has the rental reimbursement endorsement, the policy pays for
a comparable rental vehicle for up to 30 days while the insured vehicle is being
repaired following a covered claim. This benefit does not apply to liability-only
policies.

## Claims Filing Requirements
Policyholders must report an accident within 30 days of the incident. Claims must
include a description of the incident, the location, photos of vehicle damage,
and, for accidents involving another party or injury, a police report. Claims
filed after 30 days may be denied for late reporting unless good cause is shown.

## Fraud and Misrepresentation
Any claim found to include material misrepresentation, staged damage, inflated
repair estimates, or fabricated documentation may be denied in full, and the
policy may be subject to cancellation and referral to the special investigations
unit. Indicators of potential fraud include inconsistent statements across
documents, repair estimates significantly above market rate for the described
damage, and a pattern of frequent claims shortly after policy inception.

## Windshield and Glass Claims
Windshield chips and cracks are covered under comprehensive coverage. Many
policies include a glass endorsement that waives the deductible for
windshield-only repairs (not full replacements) to encourage prompt repair
before a chip spreads into a full crack.

## Coverage Limits and Payout Caps
The maximum payout for any single claim is capped at the coverage limit stated on
the policy declarations page for the applicable coverage type. Claims estimated
above the coverage limit are paid up to the limit only; the policyholder is
responsible for any amount above the limit.
